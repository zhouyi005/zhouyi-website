Artist Biography

Zhouyi Zuo is a Doctor of Musical Arts candidate in Piano Accompanying and Chamber Music at the Eastman School of Music, where he studies collaborative piano under Dr. Andrew Harley with a near-full scholarship and serves as his teaching assistant. He previously earned his Bachelor’s degree in Piano Performance from the Soochow University School of Music, studying under Dr. Yingying Su and Dr. Lo-An Lin. He later received his Master’s degree in Piano Performance from the University of Kansas, studying with Dr. Yi-Yang Chen, and furthered his collaborative piano studies with Dr. Ellen Sommer and Professor Ted Taylor.

As both a solo and collaborative pianist, Zuo has performed in prominent venues such as Swarthout Recital Hall (University of Kansas), Enling Arts Center (Soochow University), and the Lied Center of Kansas. Recent appearances include the University of Kansas Honors Recital (2023), a chamber concert at the St. Lawrence Catholic Center (2024), and the “Complete Beethoven Concerto” series under the direction of Dr. Yi-Yang Chen. He was a participant at the Montecito International Music Festival and has performed in masterclasses for the Juilliard String Quartet and Professor John Parry, who described his playing as “exceptionally perfect.”

Zuo has received numerous awards, including First Prize at the 10th Steinway National Youth Piano Competition (Suzhou Division) and an Excellence Award at the national finals. He is also a laureate of the Asian Music Competition, the Shanghai International Youth Piano Competition, and the Zhuhai Hengqin iMusic International Festival. In 2023, he won Second Place in the KMTA State Piano Competition and was invited to perform in the Chinese television program Singer 2024. His performances have been praised by esteemed pianists, including Professor Li Jian, who commended his “exquisitely refined control.”

Specializing in collaborative piano and chamber repertoire, Zuo is experienced in partnering with instrumentalists and vocalists in recital, competition, and festival settings. His musical sensitivity, technical precision, and depth of expression have established him as a versatile and dedicated young artist. During his undergraduate years, he also served as Director of the Student Union’s Arts Committee at Soochow University, organizing concerts and programming. Zuo continues to broaden his artistic reach through a wide range of collaborative and solo performances, embracing the full expressive potential of the piano across diverse musical landscapes.
